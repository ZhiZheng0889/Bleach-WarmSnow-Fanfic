# Chapter — The Last Door (Death Stage 10)

## Scene 1

- **POV**: Ren
- **Location**: The corridor’s deepest point reached so far
- **Goal**: Confront what the corridor has become; decide whether to end it, bind it, or share it

Ren walked deeper than he ever had.

The corridor did not resist.

That was how he knew he was being guided.

The gray walls curved into a chamber that was not a chamber.

A space that felt like an answer waiting to be spoken.

At the center, something shimmered.

Not a person.

Not a door.

A rule.

Ren’s skin prickled.

He understood without words.

The corridor was offering him a bargain.

Make it central.

Make it official.

Make it inevitable.

In exchange, it would become stable.

And stability was the oldest temptation.

Ren’s breath sounded wrong here.

Too loud.

As if the corridor wanted him to hear himself.

As if it wanted him to remember he was a body.

Bodies paid tolls.

Ren’s nose bled again.

He wiped it with the back of his hand and kept walking.

The gray walls curved into a chamber that was not a chamber.

It was an idea shaped into space.

At the center, something shimmered.

Not a person.

Not a door.

A rule.

Ren’s skin prickled.

He understood without words.

The corridor was offering him a bargain.

Make it central.

Make it official.

Make it inevitable.

In exchange, it would become stable.

Stable crossings.

Predictable passages.

No more accidents.

No more missing.

No more “lost between.”

Ren felt his body lean toward yes.

Stability was the oldest temptation.

It promised relief.

Relief made people forgive chains.

## Scene 2

- **POV**: Ren
- **Location**: The same place
- **Goal**: Choose decentralization as an ending; refuse the corridor’s “center”

Ren thought of the basin.

How a center invited a knife.

He thought of the paper walls.

How forms became chains.

He thought of the people he had carried out of nowhere.

People who wanted safety.

People who deserved it.

Ren exhaled.

“No center,” he said.

The corridor tightened.

For a moment, Ren thought it would swallow him.

Then the shimmer changed.

Not approval.

Not defeat.

Adaptation.

The rule fractured into many small glints.

Paths.

Not one door.

Many thresholds.

Ren stumbled.

He felt the cost, immediate and intimate.

But he also felt something else.

A future where no one could own the crossing.

Not even him.

Ren stepped closer to the shimmer.

He could feel the corridor’s anticipation.

It was not human, but it was eager.

Eagerness was the most dangerous emotion.

It made you believe you deserved the outcome.

Ren closed his eyes.

He thought of the depot organizer’s list.

Of hands moving crates.

Of people refusing a stamp.

He thought of the field of bodies and the decision to dig.

He thought of the baby crying in the safehouse.

Those were not stable things.

They were messy.

They were alive.

Ren opened his eyes.

“No center,” he said.

The corridor tightened.

For a moment, Ren thought it would swallow him.

He felt pressure on his ribs.

He felt the seam inside him squeeze like a fist.

His vision went white at the edges.

Then the shimmer changed.

Not approval.

Not defeat.

Adaptation.

The rule fractured into many small glints.

Paths.

Not one door.

Many thresholds.

Ren stumbled.

He felt the cost, immediate and intimate.

His knees hit the ground.

He tasted blood.

But he also felt something else.

A future where no one could own the crossing.

Not even him.

And that, Ren realized, was the real price.

Not pain.

Not toll.

Responsibility without ownership.
