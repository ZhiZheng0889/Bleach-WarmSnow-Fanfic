## Scene 1

- **POV**: The Spirit King
- **Location**: The White Side (the ordered plane, before Seireitei has a name)
- **Goal**: Establish stable hierarchy that prevents collapse

The first thing he built was a *center*.

Not because he loved thrones.

Because he could see what happened without one.

The world unfolded around him like a sandbox waiting for a hand to press it into shape—raw grains of possibility, too loose, too eager to slide into violence. He did not need to imagine menus. They arrived anyway, as naturally as breath.

### BUILD INTERFACE — ORDER

**VISION**: Prevent infinite war.

**FUTURE-SIGHT**: Enabled (passive)
- Branches observed: uncountable
- Acceptable outcomes: few
- Common failure state: realms bleed into each other; hunger becomes law

**FOUNDATION**
- Place **Axis**: ✅
- Define **Above / Below**: ✅
- Lock **Names** to function: ✅

He touched the emptiness and named it.

The name didn’t create the thing—nothing so crude. It *told everything else how to behave around it*.

A center meant direction.

Direction meant duty.

Duty meant people could be predicted.

He had made the mistake once—far down one branch—of believing that if you gave souls freedom, they would choose peace.

They did not.

Not when the bowl was empty.

Not when grief became currency.

Not when fear found a leader.

So he built a ladder of obligation and called it stability.

### SYSTEMS DEPLOYED

**NOBLE HOUSES** (prototype)
- Purpose: distribute responsibility through inheritance
- Benefit: continuity across generations
- Risk: hoarding / entitlement

He saw that risk.

He built it anyway.

Because the alternative was a hundred thousand separate wars.

**COURT** (prototype)
- Purpose: translate power into law
- Benefit: disputes end before they become slaughter
- Risk: law becomes performance

He saw that risk.

He built it anyway.

Because the alternative was power pretending it had no rules.

He watched futures in which the “court” never formed.

He watched a captain-something—names didn’t matter yet—cut down a crowd because the crowd had no language for appeal.

He watched a forge-city ignite itself because nobody could agree who had the right to ration fuel.

He watched a child become a tyrant simply because no one was officially allowed to stop him.

The Spirit King exhaled.

A feudal ladder was ugly.

But uglier still was a world where the only ladder was the one made of bodies.

### WORLD EVENT: WALLS

He placed the first wall as gently as one lays a blanket over a sleeping blade.

Not to imprison.

To separate.

To keep the kinds of hunger from mixing until they became something worse.

**BUILD COMPLETE**: *A realm that can endure the next day.*

He looked at the blank space beyond the wall—the place where heat waited.

And he thought of the First Smith.

Friend.

Nemesis.

Maybe both were the same word, just viewed from opposite ends of a timeline.

## Scene 2

- **POV**: The First Smith
- **Location**: The Black Side (a torn portion of Greater Hell, braced into a usable world)
- **Goal**: Build survival through shared labor; weaponize pessimism about human nature

The first thing he built was a *workshop*.

Not because he loved hammers.

Because he had seen what happened without them.

When the Warm Snow came in his old world, the nobles built pantries.

The survivors built kitchens.

One stored.

The other fed.

The First Smith set his anvil at the center of the torn-black territory and listened to it speak.

It did not speak in prophecy.

It spoke in fracture lines.

Where stone would split.

Where rage would pool.

Where a hungry crowd would become a mob.

He didn’t need to see the future.

He only needed to remember the past.

### BUILD INTERFACE — FORGE

**CORE LOOP**: Heat → Hammer → Share → Endure.

**PREDICTION MODEL**: Human nature (dark setting)
- If resource is scarce: someone will hoard.
- If a title exists: someone will abuse it.
- If fear is unspoken: someone will sell a lie as safety.

**FOUNDATION**
- Place **Forge**: ✅
- Place **Common Stores** (transparent ledgers): ✅
- Assign **Paths** (guilds, not bloodlines): ✅

He carved the first rule into the world like a chisel into hot metal:

*No soul is born above the furnace.*

Not even him.

Especially not him.

Because leaders were where the worst lies started—lies that sounded like compassion.

### SYSTEMS DEPLOYED

**PATH GUILDS**
- Purpose: coordinate labor and defense
- Benefit: skill becomes status, not blood
- Risk: guilds become mini-houses
- Countermeasure: rotate duty; publish supplies; shame secrecy

He was not naive.

He expected the guilds to try to become houses.

So he made the system fight itself.

**COMMON RATIONS**
- Purpose: keep the bowl from becoming a weapon
- Benefit: prevents starvation-based tyranny
- Risk: freeloaders / resentment
- Countermeasure: labor credits as *proof of contribution*, not currency of domination

He could already hear the clever arguments:

*But what if someone cheats?*

They would.

*But what if someone refuses to work?*

They would.

*But what if someone wants more?*

They would.

That was the point.

He built for the worst version of people because the worst version always arrived first in a disaster.

If the structure held under that weight, it would hold under anything.

### WORLD EVENT: HEAT LEAKS

The torn world tried to revert—to become Greater Hell again, endless and unworkable.

He braced it with chain-lines and furnace-runes.

Not walls.

Struts.

You didn’t pretend a forge was gentle.

You made it safe by admitting it was dangerous.

**BUILD COMPLETE**: *A realm that can endure the next night.*

He lifted his gaze toward the white side, toward the imagined court and the neat ladder of titles.

He could almost like it.

Almost admire it.

If he hadn’t seen what ladders became when the snow started falling.

Somewhere across the seam, the Spirit King was building a world that depended on people staying good.

The First Smith tapped the anvil once.

### WORLD STATE UPDATE — TWO BLUEPRINTS

**WHITE SIDE (Order)**
- The Axis holds; **Above / Below** becomes a lived truth.
- Walls exist now—separation is policy, not accident.
- “Noble Houses” and “Court” aren’t just ideas; they are the first *interfaces* for obedience.

**BLACK SIDE (Forge)**
- The Forge stabilizes the torn world; survival becomes a shared loop.
- Guild Paths and transparent stores make hunger visible—and therefore manageable.
- Heat still leaks, but the world learns to live with danger instead of denying it.

*I’ll build one that survives when they don’t.*
