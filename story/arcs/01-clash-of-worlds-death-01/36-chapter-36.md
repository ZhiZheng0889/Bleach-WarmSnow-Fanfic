## Scene 1

- **POV**: A Cinderbound marshal
- **Location**: A narrow bridge over a canal of refuse
- **Goal**: Hold a choke point without slaughter; realize the rules of the world are changing

The bridge should have been easy.

Two barricades.

A canal.

A line of Compact enforcers too tired to stand straight.

The marshal had won bridges before.

He had won streets.

He had won hinges.

He had not won weather.

A runner whispered.

“Blades are dulling,” she said.

The marshal blinked.

“From use?”

The runner shook her head.

“No,” she said. “From nothing.”

He lifted his blade.

It felt heavy.

Not weight.

Reluctance.

Steel that did not want to be steel.

He swung once at a rope.

The rope did not cut.

It frayed.

It squealed.

It resisted like it had decided to be important.

The world was misbehaving.

He had grown used to small misbehavior.

A lock that jammed.

A cart wheel that cracked.

Now it was his own tools.

The Compact enforcers advanced.

They held batons and papers.

Warrants like shields.

They shouted.

“Bandits!”

The word hit the crowd behind him.

He felt people flinch.

Labels cut.

Sometimes deeper than steel.

He stepped forward.

“No,” he said.

He lifted his blade.

It trembled.

Not in his hand.

In itself.

Then it snapped.

A clean break.

A sound like a vow being canceled.

The marshal stared at the broken steel.

For the first time in weeks, he did not feel victorious.

He felt watched.

By something that did not care who ruled.

## Scene 2

- **POV**: Lady Gao Yue
- **Location**: A temporary Audit tribunal inside a marble hall
- **Goal**: Turn chaos into precedent; punish the idea of refusal

The tribunal was a theater with paperwork instead of actors.

Lady Gao Yue liked it.

It made violence feel clean.

A man knelt in front of the table.

His hands were bound.

His mouth was not.

“I took grain,” he said. “My children—”

“Banditry,” the clerk announced.

Lady Gao Yue tilted her head.

“Did you have a stamp?” she asked.

The man shook his head.

“I had hunger,” he whispered.

Lady Gao Yue smiled.

“Hunger is not authorization,” she said.

She turned to the captain.

“Public,” she ordered.

The captain hesitated.

“Lady, the city is—”

“Public,” she repeated.

Because fear worked best when shared.

A messenger stumbled in.

“Marshal sighted,” he gasped. “Bridge engagement. His blade broke.”

Lady Gao Yue’s eyes narrowed.

“Broke?”

The messenger nodded, frightened.

“As if the steel—”

“Enough,” she snapped.

She stared at her own hands.

Steel broke.

Ink lifted.

Names fled.

The world had begun to refuse its materials.

That was not revolution.

That was omen.

She did not believe in gods.

But she believed in leverage.

If the world was changing, the Compact must be the first to claim the meaning of it.

She stood.

“Announce that the heavens punish bandits,” she said.

The clerk blinked.

“That’s…not doctrine,” he whispered.

Lady Gao Yue’s voice became ice.

“Then write doctrine,” she replied.

## Scene 3

- **POV**: The Spirit King
- **Location**: Where tools decide whether to obey
- **Goal**: Admit the friction has escalated beyond intention; choose whether to intervene directly

He had turned friction into misbehavior.

He had wanted to prevent a single hand from closing the loop.

He had wanted to keep crowns from forming.

He had not wanted steel to break in a man’s hand.

He had not wanted hunger to become a tribunal.

The world was not a machine.

It was a living compromise.

When you touched one part, the rest answered.

He felt the seam widen again.

The hungry realm pressed.

More mouths.

More absence.

He could close it.

He could tighten reality until nothing slipped.

That would mean a single ruler.

A single answer.

A stamped peace.

He could do it.

And he could watch the city suffocate under it.

He exhaled.

Intervention was also a crown.

He did not want to be worshiped.

But he did not want to be devoured either.

The first clash was breaking its own rules.

Soon it would break the world’s.

He listened to the bridge.

To the tribunal.

To the kitchens.

Then he heard something new.

A sound like a door being chewed.
