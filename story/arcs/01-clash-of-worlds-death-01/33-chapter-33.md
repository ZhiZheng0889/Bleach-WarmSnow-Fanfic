## Scene 1

- **POV**: A Cinderbound marshal
- **Location**: A looted street turned makeshift clinic
- **Goal**: Keep the Cinderbound from becoming bandits; keep the pressure without the stain

They had taken the street.

They had not taken the court.

So the street tried to take them.

A cart lay overturned.

Grain spilled.

Hands fought over it with the silent intensity of drowning.

The marshal stepped into the crush.

No speech.

No banner.

Only his presence.

He lifted his blade and planted its tip into the stone.

A small, sharp sound.

Attention turned.

“Back,” he said.

A man laughed.

“Back to what?” the man spat.

The marshal stared at him.

“To being people,” he replied.

The man’s laugh cracked.

“People is a word the Compact owns,” he said.

The marshal felt that.

Not as insult.

As diagnosis.

A runner arrived.

“Ration points reopened,” she said. “Strict Audit. Batons.”

The marshal’s jaw tightened.

Gao Yue was doing what courts did.

Turning needs into procedures.

Turning procedures into weapons.

He looked at the spilled grain.

At the hands.

At the rage.

Continuous victory was not a streak if it ended in ugliness.

He knelt.

Picked up a handful of grain.

Let it run through his fingers.

“Listen,” he said.

They listened because he was armed.

That was the sin of it.

He hated that too.

“We don’t take from each other,” he said. “We take from the locks.”

A woman with a bloody lip stared.

“And if the locks are on people?” she asked.

The marshal’s silence was long.

Then:

“Then we cut the lock without cutting the person,” he said.

It sounded like a vow.

Vows were dangerous.

They stuck.

He gestured to the cart.

“Bring bowls,” he ordered. “Bring cloth. Bring water.”

His fighters moved.

Not to seize.

To sort.

To portion.

To act like a system without pretending to be one.

He glanced toward the white court.

Refusal was not the end.

It was a delay.

Delays cost lives.

## Scene 2

- **POV**: A Compact clerk
- **Location**: The Registry hall’s smoking archive
- **Goal**: Destroy records before the enemy can seize them; preserve power without proof

The hall still burned.

Not with flame.

With heat.

Heat that made ink sweat.

Heat that made paper curl like frightened tongues.

The clerk’s hands were black with soot.

He carried a stack of ledgers toward the furnace.

A superior watched.

“Faster,” the superior said.

The clerk swallowed.

“These are births,” he whispered.

The superior did not blink.

“Then they are liabilities,” she replied.

He stared at the neat columns.

Names.

Amounts.

Debts.

Permissions.

Everything the Compact claimed to be reality.

If it burned, reality would have to improvise.

That frightened him more than the Cinderbound.

He fed the ledgers into the furnace.

A sound like dry leaves.

A scent like childhood lessons turning to smoke.

As the paper caught, the ink did something wrong.

It did not smear.

It lifted.

Threads of black rose like tiny worms.

The clerk recoiled.

“What—”

The superior’s eyes narrowed.

“Keep burning,” she snapped.

The clerk obeyed.

But he watched.

Because the ink was not just ink anymore.

It was trying to leave.

Trying to become a thing.

On the floor, a spill of ash gathered into a shape.

Not a person.

A mouth.

A hungry curve.

The clerk’s throat went dry.

“Lady,” he whispered.

The superior looked.

For the first time, her posture broke.

The ash-mouth opened.

No sound.

But the room grew colder.

And in the cold, the clerk heard a distant scraping.

Like something with no lungs trying to breathe.

## Scene 3

- **POV**: The Spirit King
- **Location**: The seam between breaths
- **Goal**: Realize the city’s conflict is pulling on the world’s oldest partition

Names were anchors.

Not because people were important to courts.

But because names held meaning in place.

Meaning was structure.

Structure was a wall.

When the Compact burned its own names, it loosened the wall.

When the Cinderbound refused to become a new wall, it left the opening open.

When Ren taught people to live without names, he taught them to slip through.

Slip.

Slip.

The seam listened.

It widened.

He felt the first thing crawl through.

Not a soldier.

Not a noble.

Not a hero.

A hunger without a body.

A mouth made of absence.

He had seen such mouths before.

In the oldest shadows.

In the places where souls forgot themselves.

He had kept them contained by keeping the world whole.

A whole world had pressure.

Pressure kept the hungry pressed flat.

But a whole world was also a single wound.

And the city was rubbing salt into it.

He tasted the ash again.

The older ash.

He opened his eyes.

The first clash was no longer only politics.

It was cosmology.

And cosmology did not tolerate improvisation.
